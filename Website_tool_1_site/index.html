Concept Map

The system is modeled as a well-mixed reactor where CO is generated by candle combustion and removed by ventilation. Key relationships include emission rate, room volume, and air changes per hour (ACH).

CO Exposure Tool

This tool estimates carbon monoxide (CO) buildup from a burning candle in an indoor room using a well-mixed (CSTR) air model with ventilation. The user inputs the number of wicks, room volume, exposure time, and fan operation to simulate ventilation effects. The tool outputs CO concentration in both mg/m¬≥ and ppm and compares the result against EPA, OSHA, and WHO exposure limits over an 8 hour time period. Users should interpret higher values or red indicators as potentially unsafe conditions requiring improved ventilation or reduced emission sources. Press the "Fan ON/OFF" button to toggle ventilation.

Discussion

The assumptions that mattered the most in this model were the fan ventilation rate and passive diffusion. Another assumption that mattered a lot was the emission rate generated by the candle. There was a lot of uncertainty for a possible value for this parameter. The model was very sensitive to this parameter. Ultimately an average value was selected which was provided by ChatGPT. These assumptions we the most important because they could vary significantly depending on the circumstance. I am rather confident in the structure and reasoning behind this tool however I am a little surprised with the concentration results often being out of compliance. I suspect a decent amount of error in the model. Many attributes to the actual system were excluded to help simplify the model which may have contributed to this result. In addition the safety standards were for exposure longer than 8 hours and the model does not approach this time scale.

AI had a significant impact on this tool. It did the majority of the heavy lifting for code generation and structure. It helped me brainstorm and draft a model especially for the decay rate due to ventilation and the rate of generation of CO. I selected the parameters to include in the model as well as the results I wanted to display. I wanted to add safety standards as a way to assess the hazard of the CO concentration. CLAUDE and ChatGPT were used to develop this tool in the coding front. I provided the framework and vision for the tools scope, application, and results.
